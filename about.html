About the Archive

Work, memory, and process in one editorial system.

This site is not built as a portfolio wall. It is an archive for projects, photographs, moving image, writing, travel, daily fragments, and the notes that connect them.

A practice shaped by making and arranging.

I work across web development, interface design, image-making, media editing, and writing. Rather than separating these into isolated categories, I think of them as connected forms of editorial work.

Some projects become websites. Some become moving images, notes, or image sequences. What matters most is how they are framed, read, remembered, and revisited over time.

Why archive instead of portfolio.

A traditional portfolio often privileges finished outcomes. An archive makes room for process, fragments, unfinished observations, and work that gains meaning through adjacency.

Here, a travel note can sit beside a design study, and a journal entry can resonate with a later project. The structure is intended to preserve those connections instead of flattening them.
